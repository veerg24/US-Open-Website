About the US Open


The US Open is one of the greatest tennis tournaments in the world. It is also one of the oldest tennis
tournaments for which men's singles was first contested in 1881. It is a hard court tennis tournament which takes place annually
spanning for about two weeks (late August to mid September). The tournament is the last grand slam tournament
of the year and is held at the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center in Flushing Meadows-Corona Park, Queens, near New York City.

Tips and Tricks

I have been to four consecutive US Opens (2014-2017) and I have to say that they are always the greatest highlight of my year.
Whether you know tennis or no, you are bound to have a great time at the tournament. But there are some tips and tricks in order to
have more fun and watch some great high quality tennis.

Tip #1: The Best Quality Matches Are Not Always On Ashe

I am a huge die-hard tennis player and fan myself and watching quality tennis is the most important thing for me when I visit the open every year.
You should do whatever you can to watch great players and matches up close. While Arthur Ashe might seem like the ideal place to watch a tennis match,
it actually doesn't always have the highest quality tennis (unless you are willing to spend a lot of money to buy tickets for the later stages in the tournament). In 2016,
I was presented with a ticket (About $300)into Ashe stadium so I would be guaranteed to watch a top 10 player. The seat was located on the second level (little bit below half way).
While it was a spectacular to experience the atmosphere and the stadium, I was still somewhat far away from the court and the players.
Even the players look tiny, especially the further up you sit. Now, you might still be thinking how does the view from the seat affect the
fact that the best quality matches are not on Ashe. The tournament committee will always put first big stars (Rafael Nadal, Roger Federer etc.) on Ashe
rather than what would be a better match. This usually results in big stars defeating their opponents with ease without many magnificent points
(only exception is if you aren't on a budget and are willing to spend a lot of money to purchase good seats close to the court for rounds such as the 3rd round or above).
As the 2016 tournament ended, I came to the conclusion that the smaller courts are fantastic for watching great players and excellent quality matches up close.


Based on this observation, I got to see top 30 players playing some extremely good tennis in epic matches on small courts
(Louis Armstrong, Courts #4-#16, and Court #17, the coolest court that is below ground level) from just a
couple of feet away in 2017. (That is a picture of Lucas Pouille after winning his match taken by me from my seat)



Picture of Genie Bouchard and Jelena Ostapenko (2017 French Open Winner) discussing their plan for the next point (taken from my seat in the first row)

Tip #2: Autographs and Photographs

If you are interested in getting players autographs and taking photos with them, the smaller courts are your best friends. Although it might seem
difficult to get autographs, it can be quite simple if you make the right decisions. Towards the end of the match, make sure to be sitting either
behind their chair (although it gets extremely crowded because everybody gathers there) or near the place where the players exit
(recommend sitting near the exit for courts except for Ashe, Louis Armstong, New Grandstand).

Tip #3: Do Not Drive Unless it's really Necessary

Driving and parking can be a real pain when there are hundreds of thousands of other people with the same idea. It would be quite a challenge
to find parking and with the insane amount of people present, the rates would most certainly increase. I would strongly recommend taking a
train to the Park. The station is located very conveniently near the east gate. Another excellent option is taking a hotel shuttle
(if your hotel provides it) to the open. Shuttles are convenient since they usually run every 30 mins and drop you off at a
specific area near the south gate.


Hotel Shuttle

Now that you have reached the open, you still need a way to get back to your hotel or house. Do not try to call an Uber. With thousands of other people
trying to call an Uber, it can get crazy to call an Uber. Also the cost of the Uber will jump up due to the rush. I would suggest returning in either the train
or the shuttle again. The shuttle continues to run until very late at night, but keep in my mind the time of the last stop the shuttle makes to the open
or else you will have no other option than to either take the train, call an Uber, or try to catch a taxi which is hectic.
